Behind the Scenes
HitMeUp was a project made for CSE 110, Software Engineering where groups spent a quarter making an application that other students would use. My group, SudoWoodo created HitMeUp, a webapp that lets you know when your friends are free. We were debating which platform to launch HitMeUp and eventually settled on a web app over mobile. This was due to the fact that some people had Androids while other had iPhones, but everyone had access to the internet. In addition, it would be much easier to update calendars over the internet.

Throughout the quarter, we met up about 4 times each week. We divided up tasks and tried our best to adhere to the agile development process. Personally, I worked primarily on the calendar. I constructed the models, wrestled with the restless api, and worked extensively on the recurring events logic. Through this class and working with my wonderful teammates, I learned many things about web development as well as working in teams.

Two of the most technical things I learned from this project were Django/ Python and how websites worked in general. By working first hand, I was able to follow how requests were handled and how users were linked to the correct pages.

When it comes to interpersonal lessons, I've found it crucial to communicate often and effectively with teammates. Dividing up tasks and making sure everyone was on the same page was crucial for us to move forward as one team.

Behind the Scenes
Toon Cutter was my final project in CSE 167: Introduction to Computer Graphics. We had to implement two new features not covered in class so I chose to implement object collision and toon shading. I also decided to use a concept known as scene graphs which was covered in a previous lecture. When I saw the list of features we could use, toon shading immediately caught my eye. Drawing the connection to toon link, I decided that I wanted him to hit something, causing me to eventually settle on object collision as the other feature.
