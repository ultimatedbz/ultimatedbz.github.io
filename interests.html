In my opinion, singing is a great way to express one's own feelings, thoughts and emotions. Interestingly, I also believe that it is a way to experience the emotions, thoughts, and feelings of other people, experiences I may never have on my own.
In the summer of 2015, I decided that I wanted to get better at singing and started taking singing lessons.

Badminton
In high school, my main priority was to become the number one singles player in my age division in the nation.
While my competitors started playing in middle school, many even earlier, I started playing my freshman year and began to train my sophomore year. That added more to the challenge of becoming number one, and I loved it.
I dedicating my high school life to badminton, playing any moment I could, thinking about it when I couldn't, and religiously watching all the videos of idol, 林丹.
I went as far as downloading every one of 林丹's matches, watching his matches frame by frame, and breaking down all his shots and footwork. Because he was left handed and I was right handed, I even mirrored all his videos to make him right handed!
My accomplishments include 2013 MVP, Glen A. Wilson High School badminton team captain 2013, League Finals Men's Singles & Men's Doubles Champion, and winner of many local tournaments. My highest rank in the USA Badminton Junior Rankings (U19 Division) was 15th.
Although the highest rank I ever got was 15th, there were a lot more lessons that I learned. My passion and love for the sport taught me a lot about hard work. Badminton taught me at an early age that success needs to be earned. Through badminton, I've also learned to persevere under pressure .
Most importantly, the sport brought many new people into life, many of whom are still my closest friends to this day.
